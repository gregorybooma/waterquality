MIT Sea Grant Water Quality Data Service

With this Webservice, you can store, manage, share, and explore ocean-related data. You should find it useful and interesting if:
  • You are a scientist, manager, educator, citizen scientist, or student.
  • You are a person interested in the ocean and its processes.
  • You are a data nut.
  • Terms like "interoperability", "metadata", and "quality assurance" cause you a deep ennui.
  • Any combination of the above.
Using the service is easy:
  1. Use our interactive map to indicate general locations where you record data. If you have precise geographic coordinates for your measurements, you can include those as variables in your data.
  2. Create a new event for the location. This could be anything from a momentary observation to a long-term deployment of a data logger.
  3. Select your event, indicate the names and column order of variables in your data table, confirm your settings, and upload the data.
  4. Create customized tables and graphs via our map-based interface.
Anyone can explore and download the data on our site, but to upload data you will need to create an account (it's painless, really.) Get started:
  • Explore and enter data
  • Create an account
